Plot Overview

Severance is a science fiction psychological thriller series on Apple TV+, created by Dan Erickson and directed by Ben Stiller and Aoife McArdle, set at Lumon Industries in the fictional town of Kier. The series explores a severance procedure that surgically divides employees’ work (“innies”) and personal (“outies”) memories, focusing on Mark Scout (Adam Scott), a former history professor leading the Macrodata Refinement (MDR) team. Season 1 (2022) follows Mark and his team—Helly Riggs (Britt Lower), Dylan George (Zach Cherry), and Irving Bailiff (John Turturro)—as they navigate Lumon’s sterile, maze-like severed floor, sorting “scary numbers.” Helly’s rebellion and Petey Kilmer’s reintegration spark a conspiracy, culminating in an “overtime contingency” breach, exposing innies to the outside world at a gala, Mark’s home, and Burt’s doorstep, revealing Ms. Casey (Dichen Lachman) as Mark’s supposedly dead wife, Gemma. The season ends on a cliffhanger, with the team’s worlds irreversibly altered.

Season 2 (2025), picking up after the breach, sees Mark return to Lumon under pressure from Seth Milchick, facing new MDR members and a falsified narrative of fame. It delves into the fallout, introducing characters like Gwendoline Christie’s goat-tending Mammalian and deepening the mystery of severance’s purpose through Mark’s search for Helly, reunions with Burt, and uncovering secrets like the goat room and “Cold Harbor” project. The season progresses with Mark infiltrating the testing floor, confronting a preteen manager, and testing severance limits, all while Cobel plots revenge. It ends with Mark torn between saving Gemma or joining Helly’s rebellion against Jame Eagan’s expansion plans, leaving a cliffhanger as the innies fight for control, with Season 3 confirmed on March 21, 2025. The show blends influences from The Stanley Parable, Office Space, and Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind, winning Emmys for Main Title Design and Musical Score in 2022.

Trivia: Inspired by Erickson’s office job woes, the series has been praised for its unique premise and atmospheric design.

Characters

Main Characters

  • Mark Scout (Adam Scott): MDR team leader, a widower who severed to escape grief over Gemma’s “death,” curious yet compliant innie, depressed outie.
  • Helly Riggs / Helena Eagan (Britt Lower): New MDR hire, innie resists fiercely, outie supports severance publicly, revealed as an Eagan family member.
  • Dylan George (Zach Cherry): MDR member obsessed with perks like finger traps, fiercely loyal but shaken by breaches.
  • Irving Bailiff (John Turturro): Devout Lumon follower, enamored with Burt, struggles with innie-outie conflicts.
  • Harmony Cobel / Mrs. Selvig (Patricia Arquette): Unsevered MDR boss, monitors Mark as neighbor, fired after Season 1, returns with vengeance.
  • Seth Milchick (Tramell Tillman): Unsevered supervisor, enforces rules with a smile, conducts rituals like the Dance of the Four Tempers.
  • Burt Goodman (Christopher Walken): Optics & Design head, Irving’s love interest, retires but faces tension.
  • Ms. Casey / Gemma Scout (Dichen Lachman): Wellness counselor, Mark’s severed wife presumed dead, works in testing floor.

Supporting Characters

  • Petey Kilmer (Yul Vazquez): Ex-colleague who reintegrates, warns Mark, dies from process.
  • Ricken Hale (Michael Chernus): Mark’s eccentric brother-in-law, author of “The You You Are,” aids innies unknowingly.
  • Devon Hale (Jen Tullock): Mark’s sister, Ricken’s wife, gives birth to Eleanor in Season 1.
  • Gwendoline Christie’s Character (Mammalian): Season 2 addition, tends goats, purpose tied to Lumon experiments.
  • Sarah Bock’s Character: Season 2 regular, role in MDR leadership, details TBD.
  • Merritt Wever’s Character: Season 2 addition, tragic figure tied to Lumon’s past.
  • Mr. Graner (David Costabile): Lumon security chief, killed by Wellness post-breach.
  • Natalie (Sydney Cole Alexander): Lumon PR rep, delivers Cobel’s firing news.
  • Jame Eagan (James Urbaniak): Current CEO, Helly’s father, pushes severance propaganda.
  • Gabriel “Gabe” Scout (Nolan Green): Mark and Gemma’s unborn child, referenced in sessions.
  • Rebeck “Reghabi” (Karen Aldridge): Doctor reintegrating Petey, assists Mark’s outie.
  • Angelo Arteta (Ethan Flower): Senator pushing severance legalization, tied to Lumon.
  • Wadsworth “Waddy” (Mark Kenneth Smaltz): MDR member replacing Helly in Season 2 premiere.
  • Chadwick “Chad” (Michael Cumpsty): Another new MDR member in Season 2.
  • Kier Eagan (Marc Geller): Lumon founder, godlike figure, appears in animatronics.
  • Baldwin Eagan (Judith Lightfoot Clarke): Historical Eagan, featured in Perpetuity Wing.
  • Myrtle Eagan (Annie McNamara): Young girl in flashbacks, early test subject.
  • Felton (Mark Fairchild): Minor Lumon employee, assists Milchick.
Season 1 Episodes

Episode 1: Good News About Hell (Feb 18, 2022)

The series opens with Mark Scout finding Helly Riggs unconscious on a conference table at Lumon Industries, marking her chaotic introduction to the severed floor. As the MDR team leader, he guides her through the disorienting onboarding process, explaining how severance splits their work and personal memories into separate lives. Helly’s initial bewilderment mirrors Mark’s own suppressed grief over his wife Gemma’s supposed death, though he masks it with a veneer of corporate cheerfulness. Outside, a disheveled Petey Kilmer, Mark’s former colleague who has somehow reintegrated his memories, approaches him in a parking lot and slips him a cryptic card hinting at Lumon’s dark underbelly. This encounter plants the first seeds of doubt in Mark, setting the stage for a slow unraveling of the company’s sterile facade.

Trivia: Filmed at the Bell Labs Holmdel Complex, its brutalist architecture enhances the oppressive atmosphere.

Episode 2: Half Loop (Feb 18, 2022)

Helly struggles to adapt to MDR’s repetitive task of sorting "scary numbers" on a vintage computer, her defiance clashing with Mark’s efforts to maintain team morale in their windowless office. Dylan revels in minor company perks like finger traps, while Irving recites Lumon doctrine from the employee handbook, showcasing their individual ways of coping with the monotony. Mark discovers Petey’s hand-drawn map hidden inside a photo frame at his desk, sparking curiosity about the company’s labyrinthine layout beyond their department. Outside, Mark attends a joyless dinner at his brother-in-law Ricken and sister Devon’s home, his outie’s isolation and depression stark against their quirky warmth. The episode ends with Helly’s repeated question “Who are you?” to Mark, underlining the existential divide between their innies and outies.

Trivia: Helly’s “Who are you?” echoes the pilot’s theme of identity loss.

Episode 3: In Perpetuity (Feb 25, 2022)

Helly attempts to escape by smashing a window with a chair, only to be caught and sent to a wellness session with the serene yet unsettling Ms. Casey, who probes her psyche with abstract questions about her innie’s emotions. Mark leads the team on a field trip to the Perpetuity Wing, a museum-like shrine to Lumon founder Kier Eagan, where animatronic figures recite the company’s history, unnerving Helly further. Dylan and Irving marvel at the displays, their awe revealing how deeply Lumon’s propaganda has taken root in their severed minds. Outside, Mark’s outie visits Petey’s grave after learning of his death, grappling with fragmented memories stirred by their brief encounter, his sorrow palpable. The episode contrasts the team’s growing unease with Lumon’s reverence for its past, hinting at the corporation’s pervasive control.

Trivia: Introduces Kier’s “Four Tempers” (Woe, Frolic, Dread, Malice), a cornerstone of Lumon culture.

Episode 4: The You You Are (Mar 4, 2022)

Irving meets Burt Goodman from the Optics & Design department during a chance encounter in the hallways, sparking a tender romance that defies Lumon’s sterile environment. Outside, Mark’s outie reads Ricken’s self-help book “The You You Are” left on his doorstep, its simplistic platitudes piercing his emotional numbness and stirring thoughts of Gemma’s loss. Inside, Helly submits a resignation request via the company’s system, only to receive a video from her outie coldly rejecting it, deepening her innie’s sense of entrapment. The team continues their data refinement under Seth Milchick’s watchful supervision, the mundane task contrasting with the personal revelations unfolding outside Lumon’s walls. The episode bridges Mark’s two worlds through Ricken’s book, exploring themes of identity and connection amidst severance’s isolation.

Trivia: Ricken’s book becomes a pivotal plot device, later aiding the innies’ rebellion.

Episode 5: The Grim Barbarity of Optics and Design (Mar 11, 2022)

The MDR team ventures beyond their usual workspace, stumbling upon a room with baby goats tended by an unseen figure, leaving Mark and Helly puzzled about its purpose within Lumon. Irving visits Burt in Optics & Design, where their team crafts propaganda like Kier’s portraits, deepening their bond amidst the corporate oddity. During a wellness session, Ms. Casey evokes buried memories of Gemma in Mark’s innie, intensifying his unease and hinting at a connection he can’t fully grasp. Dylan fixates on earning a waffle party as a reward for hitting a data quota, his perk-driven enthusiasm clashing with Helly’s growing rebellion against their confinement. The episode’s surreal elements, particularly the goats, foreshadow Lumon’s broader, unsettling agenda that will unfold in Season 2.

Trivia: The goats foreshadow Gwendoline Christie’s Season 2 role as the Mammalian.

Episode 6: Hide and Seek (Mar 18, 2022)

Harmony Cobel, the unsevered MDR boss, unexpectedly provides the team with restricted files exposing Lumon’s surveillance practices, aligning with them against Milchick’s strict oversight. Helly decodes the files, uncovering hints of the company’s motives behind severance, while Mark tries to temper her zeal with caution to avoid punishment. Outside, Mark attends Petey’s funeral, where Dr. Rebeck “Reghabi” warns him of reintegration’s fatal risks, shaking his outie’s resolve to stay ignorant. Irving’s loyalty to Lumon wavers as he pines for Burt, caught between corporate duty and personal desire, his handbook recitation faltering. The episode builds tension as the team’s defiance grows, setting the stage for a seismic shift in their understanding of Lumon.

Trivia: Filmed in Nyack, NY, adding a somber realism to Petey’s farewell scene.

Episode 7: Defiant Jazz (Mar 25, 2022)

Dylan discovers and activates the “overtime contingency” protocol in the MDR control room, allowing innies to surface outside work hours, disrupting Milchick’s authority with an impromptu jazz dance meant to distract them. Mark’s innie awakens at Devon’s home during her baby shower, meeting Ms. Casey and realizing she’s Gemma, shattering his belief in her death with a flood of emotion. Helly emerges at a Lumon gala, poised to speak as attendees watch, while Irving finds himself at Burt’s doorstep, their reunion tender yet fraught. The breaches expose the team to their outies’ lives—Mark’s shock, Helly’s defiance, Irving’s longing—unraveling Lumon’s grip on their minds. The episode peaks with chaotic revelations, blending despair and hope as the severance barrier crumbles.

Trivia: Milchick’s bizarre dance symbolizes Lumon’s desperate grasp on control.

Episode 8: What’s for Dinner? (Apr 1, 2022)

The MDR team earns a waffle party for hitting a data milestone, a grotesque reward where Dylan indulges in waffles and erotic dancing while Mark and Helly finalize their breach plan. Cobel defends Lumon’s mission to security chief Mr. Graner, her devotion to Kier Eagan evident as she clutches a childhood artifact, clashing with the team’s rebellion. Milchick oversees the ritual with forced cheer, his unease growing as the innies prepare to activate the overtime contingency again. Outside, Mark’s outie nears a breakdown, haunted by Gemma’s absence and Petey’s death, unaware she’s alive within Lumon’s walls. The episode’s absurdity—waffles amid conspiracy—culminates in the team’s bold move to defy severance, setting up the season’s explosive finale.

Trivia: The waffle party nods to corporate absurdity, a darkly comedic highlight.

Episode 9: The We We Are (Apr 8, 2022)

The overtime contingency fully activates, thrusting the innies into the outside world: Helly crashes a Lumon gala, denouncing severance in a speech as CEO Jame Eagan watches in fury. Mark awakens at home, confronting Cobel (posing as Mrs. Selvig) and accusing her of hiding Gemma, his anger boiling over. Irving appears at Burt’s doorstep, their love tested by severance’s divide, while Dylan finds himself beside his wife Gretchen, overwhelmed by his outie’s domestic life. The breaches expose Lumon’s lies in a climactic trifecta—Helly’s public defiance, Mark’s discovery of Gemma as Ms. Casey, and Irving’s bittersweet reunion—shattering the team’s isolation. The season ends on a cliffhanger, with Mark shouting “She’s alive!” as the innies’ worlds irreversibly collide with their outies’.

Trivia: An Emmy-nominated finale, its triple reveal stunned audiences worldwide.

Season 2 Episodes

Episode 1: Hello, Ms. Cobel (Jan 17, 2025)

Mark Scout awakens on Lumon’s severed floor after the Season 1 revolt, disoriented as Seth Milchick greets him with a falsified newspaper proclaiming him a celebrity for his “loyalty” to the company. His original MDR team—Helly, Dylan, and Irving—has been replaced by new members Wadsworth “Waddy” and Chadwick “Chad,” who regard him with a mix of suspicion and deference under Lumon’s new regime. Harmony Cobel, reinstated despite her Season 1 firing, looms over the floor, her dual identity as Mrs. Selvig now an open threat as she watches Mark closely. Outside, Mark’s outie reels from the revelation that Gemma is alive as Ms. Casey, torn between relief and fury at Lumon’s deception, his resolve hardening. The episode sets a tense, oppressive tone, with Mark isolated yet determined to uncover the truth behind his team’s fate and Lumon’s lies.

Trivia: Filmed in a Grand Central Station replica, amplifying Lumon’s Orwellian scale.

Episode 2: Goodbye, Mrs. Selvig (Jan 24, 2025)

Mark searches Lumon’s twisting corridors for Helly and Gemma, evading Milchick’s tightened security measures implemented after the gala breach exposed severance’s flaws. Cobel’s firing is reversed by the board, but her authority wanes following Graner’s death, fueling her vendetta against Mark as she leverages her Selvig persona to spy on his outie. Outside, Dylan’s outie meets his wife Gretchen for the first time since the breach, her warmth contrasting sharply with his perk-obsessed innie, stirring a newfound resolve to fight Lumon. Irving, separated from Burt after their doorstep reunion, clings to fading memories of their bond, his once-unshakable faith in Kier Eagan shaken by severance’s toll. The episode escalates the team’s fragmentation, with Mark’s desperate quest driving the narrative toward unraveling Lumon’s next move.

Trivia: Gretchen’s name, revealed in teasers, suggests a deeper exploration of outie lives.

Episode 3: The Goat Room (Jan 31, 2025)

Mark leads Waddy and Chad into a hidden Lumon basement, discovering the goat room where Gwendoline Christie’s character, known only as the Mammalian, tends a herd of baby goats with an eerie calm, hinting at bizarre experiments tied to severance. Irving reunites with Burt outside during a rare moment of freedom, their tender embrace marred by Burt’s outie rejecting their innie connection, leaving Irving heartbroken. Dylan grapples with his outie’s domestic life with Gretchen, his innie’s fixation on perks fading as he seeks a greater purpose against Lumon’s control. Helly, absent from MDR, sends a coded message through Waddy’s workstation—“Keep looking”—urging Mark to persist despite her unknown whereabouts. The episode deepens Lumon’s mysteries, with the goats’ presence suggesting a disturbing purpose that ties back to the company’s biotech ambitions.

Trivia: The goats, teased since Season 1, connect directly to Christie’s enigmatic Season 2 role.

Episode 4: Reunions (Feb 7, 2025)

Helly’s innie resurfaces at a Lumon fundraising event, clashing publicly with her outie Helena Eagan’s pro-severance speech, exposing deep family tensions as Jame Eagan, her father, watches with cold fury. Mark uncovers technical schematics of the severance implant from files stolen during a late-night MDR shift, sharing them with Chad to build trust among the new team. Outside, Ricken delivers a second self-help book to Mark’s doorstep, its words—“You are enough”—echoing into his innie’s resolve to fight Lumon’s lies. Milchick doubles security patrols after spotting Helly’s return, while Cobel manipulates Jame into tightening control over the severed floor to thwart further rebellion. The episode pits Helly’s dual identities against each other, amplifying the stakes of Lumon’s propaganda war.

Trivia: The Eagan family dynamic expands, rooted in Helly’s Season 1 revelation as Helena.

Episode 5: Testing Floor Secrets (Feb 14, 2025)

Ms. Casey’s role on Lumon’s testing floor expands as she conducts memory experiments on severed employees, revealing Gemma’s fragmented recollections of Mark from before her “death” in a staged car accident. Mark infiltrates the testing floor with Waddy’s reluctant help, witnessing Gemma’s vacant stare as she sorts objects in a sterile room, vowing to free her from Lumon’s grasp. Merritt Wever debuts as June Kilmer, Petey’s grieving sister, who confronts Mark’s outie outside, blaming Lumon for her brother’s death and urging him to expose the truth. Devon comforts Mark’s outie at home, unaware of Gemma’s survival, as he wrestles with guilt over his ignorance of her fate. The episode blends personal tragedy with corporate horror, spotlighting Gemma’s limbo and June’s quest for justice.

Trivia: A standalone backstory episode, confirmed by Wever’s casting as June Kilmer.

Episode 6: The Preteen Manager (Feb 21, 2025)

A preteen deputy manager named Ellie is assigned to oversee MDR, her youthful authority unnerving Mark, Waddy, and Chad with her robotic recitation of Lumon protocols despite her age. Mark smuggles encrypted messages to Helly, who’s confined to a reeducation wing after her gala outburst, risking detection by Milchick’s ever-present surveillance. Dylan rallies Waddy and Chad into a fragile alliance against Ellie’s propaganda, his outie’s bond with Gretchen fueling his innie’s defiance. Episode 7: Severance Limits (Feb 28, 2025)

Mark and Helly, briefly reunited via a smuggled radio, test the severance implant’s limits using a stolen override device, causing fleeting memory bleed-throughs that risk exposure by Milchick’s patrols. Episode 8: Cold Harbor Revealed (Mar 7, 2025)

Lumon’s “Cold Harbor” project is revealed in hacked MDR files as a plan to expand severance nationwide, alarming Mark and Helly as they decode its biotech implications during a late shift. The team fractures when Chad succumbs to Milchick’s pressure, reporting their activities, while Waddy doubles down on resistance, stealing more files to expose the truth. Outside, Mark’s outie visits Reghabi, who warns that reintegration could kill him like it did Petey, as Gemma’s fate weighs heavily on his decision to keep fighting. Irving confronts Burt’s outie at a diner, who rejects their innie bond and reveals he’s left Lumon, plunging Irving into despair over their lost connection. The episode’s Cold Harbor bombshell sets up a late-season pivot, testing the team’s unity against Lumon’s grand scheme.

Trivia: “Cold Harbor” was first name-dropped in Episode 1, foreshadowing its significance.

Episode 9: Mark's Choice (Mar 14, 2025)

Mark discovers Kier Eagan’s original severance blueprint in the Perpetuity Wing during a clandestine search, linking it to Cold Harbor and Lumon’s endgame of mass memory control. Helly rallies Waddy and Dylan at an emergency board meeting infiltrated via hacked credentials, publicly clashing with Jame Eagan’s vision of a “severed future” as security closes in. Dylan’s outie aids the cause through Gretchen, slipping a jammer into Lumon to disrupt surveillance, while Irving wrestles with Kier’s legacy after finding a letter from Burt confessing regret. Mark faces a wrenching choice: rescue Gemma from the testing floor, where she’s deteriorating, or back Helly’s rebellion to stop Cold Harbor, torn between love and duty. The episode’s penultimate twist positions the team on the brink of upheaval, with Mark’s decision hanging in the balance.

Trivia: Echoes Season 1’s Episode 9 intensity, setting up a climactic resolution.

Episode 10: The Cliffhanger (Mar 21, 2025)

The MDR innies seize control of their floor, barricading the office as Mark chooses to join Helly’s assault on Lumon’s server room over freeing Gemma, hoping to disable Cold Harbor’s rollout. Milchick and Cobel launch a counterattack, locking down the building with armed guards, while Jame Eagan vows in a broadcast to expand severance across the country despite the uprising. Dylan and Irving hold off security in a desperate stand, their sacrifices buying time as Waddy hacks the system and Chad, guilt-ridden, switches sides to help. Outside, Mark’s outie collapses from the strain, Devon finding him unconscious as the innie breach reverberates through his mind, blurring the lines further. The season ends on a cliffhanger—servers sparking, Gemma unseen, and Mark’s fate uncertain—leaving fans eager for Season 3, confirmed on March 21, 2025.

Trivia: Matches Season 1’s finale with high-stakes drama and an open-ended close.
